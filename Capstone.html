Business Objectives

Hyster-Yale

Hyster-Yale is a leading global manufacturer of materials handling equipment and services. Founded in 1929, Hyster-Yale has grown to become one of the largest and most respected names in the industry. In its development center in Nijmegen, The Netherlands Heavy-Duty lifting trucks (Big truck) are developed. As part of its commitment to sustainability, Hyster-Yale has started developing a comprehensive program for electrifying its trucks.

Electric trucks carry less potential energy with them then the conventional ICE trucks. For example, a 16 tons fork lift has a diesel tank of 206 L which is roughly 2060 KWh, the electric variant has maximum 4 battery packs of 70KWh, which gives the truck a maximum capacity of 280 Kwh.

Because a customer ideally doesn’t want to adjust his operation, Hyster-Yale is searching where to improve efficiency. A full electric truck already has a efficiency between 85 and 95 %. This raises the question wat the impact off the operator is on the overall energy usage.

Problem definition

In the automotive industry gamification is used to make the operator aware of his energy behavior, and motivate the operator to drive more efficient.

Business question: What is the effect of the operator on the overall energy usage of the truck?

The term operator inputs are in this project limited to accelerator pedal which is the input for accelerate the truck, and hoist-lever which is used for lifting/accelerating the load vertically.
